# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in the CC Desk community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by mistakes.
- Focusing on what is best for the overall community.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. They may remove, edit, reject, or otherwise respond to contributions, comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and when an individual is officially representing CC Desk in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Until a dedicated Code of Conduct contact is published, use the repository's Private Vulnerability Reporting form and prefix the title with `[conduct]`. This is a temporary private contact channel for conduct reports; technical vulnerability reports continue to follow the [Security Policy](SECURITY.md). Maintainers will classify `[conduct]` reports separately from security reports and will not convert them into public issues. Do not include credentials, personal data beyond what is necessary to investigate, or a reproduction of abusive content unless needed for review.

Maintainers will review reports promptly and may issue a correction, warning, temporary ban, permanent ban, or other appropriate response. Retaliation against a reporter is not tolerated.
